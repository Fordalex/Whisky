Single Malt Whisky
Made from 100% malted barley and produced at a single distillery. Distilled in pot stills and aged in barrels for at least three years (Scotch law).
Flavor Profile: Complex, with regional influences (e.g., fruity, peaty, or smoky).
Blended Whisky
A mix of malt whisky (barley-based) and grain whisky (corn, wheat, or rye-based) from multiple distilleries. Carefully blended to achieve consistency and balance.
Flavor Profile: Smooth and light, appealing to a broader audience.
Grain Whisky
Made from a mix of grains (corn, wheat, or barley), often in column stills. Distilled at a higher proof, creating a lighter and more neutral spirit.
Flavor Profile: Mild and light; used in blends but also bottled as a single grain whisky.
Bourbon
American whiskey made with at least 51% corn. Aged in new, charred oak barrels and cannot contain additives.
Flavor Profile: Sweet, with notes of caramel, vanilla, and oak.
Tennessee Whiskey
Similar to Bourbon but must be made in Tennessee and use the Lincoln County Process (charcoal filtration before aging).
Flavor Profile: Sweet and mellow, with a slightly smoother finish than Bourbon.
Rye Whiskey
Made from at least 51% rye grain (American rye) or 100% rye (Canadian rye). Distilled like Bourbon but with a different mash bill focused on rye.
Flavor Profile: Spicy, peppery, with hints of dried fruit and herbs.
Scotch Whisky
Whisky made in Scotland and aged for at least three years. Includes several subtypes:
  • Single Malt: Made from malted barley at a single distillery.
  • Blended Malt: A blend of single malts from different distilleries.
  • Grain: Made from grains other than barley, often used in blends.
  • Blended Scotch: A mix of malt and grain whiskies.

Flavor Profile: Diverse; can be smoky (Islay), fruity (Speyside), or peaty (Highlands).
Single Malt Whisky
Made From: 100% malted barley.
Process: Produced at a single distillery. The barley is malted (soaked, germinated, and dried), mashed with hot water, fermented with yeast, distilled in pot stills, and aged in oak barrels for at least three years.
Steps:
  • Malting: Barley is soaked, germinated, and dried, often using peat smoke for flavor.
  • Mashing: Malted barley is mixed with hot water to extract fermentable sugars, producing "wort."
  • Fermentation: Yeast is added to the wort, converting sugars into alcohol, creating "wash."
  • Distillation: The wash is distilled twice in copper pot stills to concentrate alcohol and flavor.
  • Aging: Whisky is matured in oak barrels (often ex-bourbon or sherry casks), developing flavor over time.
Flavor Profile: Complex, with regional influences like fruity (Speyside), peaty (Islay), or floral (Highlands).
Blended Whisky
Made From: A mix of malt whisky (barley-based) and grain whisky (made from corn, wheat, or rye).
Process: Malt whiskies from various distilleries are blended with grain whiskies for balance and consistency.
Steps:
  • Distillation: Malt whiskies are distilled in pot stills; grain whiskies in column stills.
  • Aging: Both malt and grain whiskies are aged separately in barrels (minimum of three years).
  • Blending: A master blender combines whiskies to achieve a balanced, consistent flavor.
Flavor Profile: Smooth, light, and approachable, with a wide range of flavors depending on the blend.
Grain Whisky
Made From: Corn, wheat, or unmalted barley, with some malted barley added for fermentation.
Process: Distilled in continuous column stills, which are more efficient and produce a lighter spirit. Often aged in oak barrels for a minimum of three years.
Steps:
  • Grain Mashing: Grains are cooked and mashed to release fermentable sugars.
  • Continuous Distillation: The mash is distilled in a column still, producing a higher-proof spirit.
  • Aging: Aged in oak barrels, often for use in blended whiskies.
Flavor Profile: Mild and light, often with sweet or nutty notes.
Bourbon
Made From: At least 51% corn, with the remainder typically including rye, wheat, and malted barley.
Process: Distilled in the U.S. and aged in new, charred oak barrels. No additives allowed.
Steps:
  • Mashing: Corn and other grains are cooked and mashed to extract sugars.
  • Fermentation: Yeast is added to the mash, creating a sour mash with alcohol and CO2.
  • Distillation: Distilled in column stills, often followed by pot still distillation.
  • Aging: Aged in new, charred American oak barrels, developing caramel, vanilla, and smoky flavors.
Flavor Profile: Sweet, with notes of caramel, vanilla, and oak; smooth and rich.
Tennessee Whiskey
Made From: Similar to Bourbon (51% corn) but with additional charcoal filtration.
Process: Distilled in Tennessee and filtered through sugar maple charcoal before aging (Lincoln County Process).
Steps:
  • Charcoal Filtering: The raw spirit is filtered through sugar maple charcoal before barreling.
  • Aging: Aged in new, charred oak barrels to develop flavor.
Flavor Profile: Sweet and mellow, with a smoother finish than Bourbon.
Rye Whiskey
Made From: At least 51% rye grain (American rye) or 100% rye (Canadian rye).
Process: Distilled similarly to Bourbon but with rye as the dominant grain, giving it a spicier profile.
Steps:
  • Mashing: Rye and other grains are mashed to release sugars.
  • Fermentation: Yeast converts sugars into alcohol.
  • Distillation: Distilled in column stills or pot stills.
  • Aging: Aged in new, charred oak barrels (American rye) or other barrels (Canadian rye).
Flavor Profile: Spicy, peppery, with herbal and dried fruit notes.
Scotch Whisky
Made From: Malted barley (single malt) or a mix of grains (blended or grain whisky).
Process: Distilled in Scotland and aged for at least three years in oak barrels.
Steps:
  • Malting: Barley is malted and dried, sometimes over peat fires for a smoky flavor.
  • Fermentation: Yeast converts sugars into alcohol in the wash.
  • Distillation: Distilled twice in pot stills (for single malt) or in column stills (for grain whisky).
  • Aging: Aged in used barrels (e.g., Bourbon or sherry casks), often for much longer than the legal minimum.
Flavor Profile: Varies by region: smoky (Islay), fruity (Speyside), or peaty (Highlands).
